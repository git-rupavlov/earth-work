# 3-Year Development Plan

## Overview

The purpose of the first three years is to establish soil structure, organic matter reserves, biological activity, water retention, biomass production systems and operational knowledge of the site.

Food production is included, but during the establishment phase it remains a secondary output.

---

# Year 1: Assessment and Soil Building

## Objectives

- understand site conditions;
- establish baseline observations;
- begin soil improvement;
- create biomass production areas;
- establish composting operations;
- produce small quantities of food.

## Greenhouse Utilization

Target active use:

```text
100-150 m²
```

Remaining area:

```text
Observation
Cover crops
Future expansion
```

## Experimental Beds

### Bed A: Alfalfa + Amaranth + Phacelia

Purpose:

- deep rooting;
- biomass production;
- microbial stimulation;
- pollinator support.

Expected effects:

- improved soil aggregation;
- increased biological activity;
- mulch production.

### Bed B: Alfalfa + Amaranth + Clover

Purpose:

- nitrogen fixation;
- permanent soil cover;
- biomass production;
- moisture retention.

Expected effects:

- reduced evaporation;
- reduced weed pressure;
- improved nitrogen cycling.

### Bed C: Alfalfa + Amaranth + Buckwheat

Purpose:

- biomass production;
- phosphorus mobilization;
- rapid soil cover.

Expected effects:

- increased nutrient availability;
- quick canopy development;
- improved mulch production.

### Bed D: Cherry Tomatoes + Basil + Marigold + Nasturtium

Purpose:

- limited food production;
- greenhouse observation;
- pest and disease monitoring.

Metrics:

- yield;
- disease pressure;
- irrigation requirements;
- temperature response.

## Biomass Production Area

Outside greenhouse.

Target:

```text
100-300 m²
```

Potential species:

- amaranth;
- sorghum;
- alfalfa;
- sunflower;
- millet.

Objective:

- mulch production;
- compost feedstock;
- root biomass.

## Compost System

Minimum target:

```text
5 m³
```

Desired target:

```text
10+ m³
```

Feedstocks:

- manure;
- grass clippings;
- plant residues;
- leaves;
- wood chips;
- crop residues.

## Success Criteria

### Soil

- permanent soil cover established;
- first mulch cycle completed;
- active root systems present.

### Infrastructure

- compost area operational;
- water source evaluated;
- cultivation zones defined.

### Food

Small production of:

- tomatoes;
- herbs;
- cucurbits;
- leafy vegetables.

# Year 2: Expansion and Validation

## Objectives

- increase cultivated area;
- validate Year 1 observations;
- improve nutrient cycling;
- reduce external inputs.

## Greenhouse Utilization

Target:

```text
250-350 m²
```

## Cover Crop Systems

Permanent inclusion of:

- alfalfa;
- clover;
- phacelia;
- buckwheat;
- vetch.

Target:

```text
30-50% of cultivated area
```

## Food Production

Expansion of:

- tomatoes;
- peppers;
- cucumbers;
- chickpeas;
- herbs.

## Soil Monitoring

Observe:

- infiltration rates;
- earthworm activity;
- mulch decomposition;
- organic matter accumulation;
- root penetration depth.

## Compost System

Target:

```text
10-20 m³ annually
```

Internal production becomes the primary fertility source.

---

# Year 3: Stabilization and Optimization

## Objectives

- establish self-reinforcing biological cycles;
- reduce dependency on imported materials;
- optimize crop placement;
- expand only where water, biomass and labor allow.

## Greenhouse Utilization

Target:

```text
350-500 m²
```

Only if justified by:

- available water;
- available biomass;
- available labor.

## Site Functions

### Food Production

Human consumption.

### Biomass Production

Mulch and compost.

### Nutrient Cycling

Internal fertility generation.

### Observation

Continuous improvement.

## Soil Targets

### Organic Matter

Increase relative to Year 1 baseline.

### Biological Activity

Indicators:

- earthworms;
- fungal growth;
- rapid residue decomposition.

### Water Management

Reduced irrigation requirements per unit area.

---

# Long-Term Direction

The site should transition from:

```text
External Inputs
        ↓
Crop Production
```

towards:

```text
Biomass
        ↓
Compost
        ↓
Soil Improvement
        ↓
Food Production
```

The primary product of the system is fertile soil.

Food production is a consequence of soil development.
