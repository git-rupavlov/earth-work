# Plant Profiles

## Purpose

Each plant profile describes the ecological function of species within the NoMad's Farm ecosystem.

Plants are evaluated not only as food crops but also as biological tools capable of influencing:

- soil structure;
- water retention;
- nutrient cycling;
- biological activity;
- biomass production.

---

# Priority Species for 2026-2027

## Nettle (Urtica dioica)

### Primary Function

Biomass, compost feedstock and mineral accumulation.

### Ecological Functions

- high biomass production;
- mulch production;
- liquid fertilizer production;
- pollinator support;
- habitat creation.

### Project Role

Permanent biomass sector around the greenhouse.

---

## Lapad (Rumex patientia)

### Primary Function

Edible perennial biomass crop.

### Ecological Functions

- early spring food production;
- deep-root nutrient cycling;
- mulch production;
- compost feedstock;
- living soil cover.

### Project Role

Core perennial crop around the greenhouse.

---

## Alfalfa (Medicago sativa)

Deep-rooted perennial nitrogen-fixing plant.

### Project Role

Primary soil improvement and cover crop species for field development.

---

## Amaranth (Amaranthus spp.)

### Project Role

Seed production, edible biomass and mulch generation.

Target varieties:

- Red Garnet;
- Green Garnet.

---

## Butternut Squash

### Project Role

Primary food-production crop in the river field sector.

Strengths:

- high yield;
- long storage;
- soil shading;
- drought resilience.
